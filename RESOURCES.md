# LLC and C-Corp Contributions and Reorgs Resources

## Knowledge

- [IRS Publication 3402: Taxation of Limited Liability Companies](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p3402)
  Use for LLC federal tax classification basics, including disregarded entity, partnership, corporation, and later classification changes.
- [IRS Publication 541: Partnerships](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p541)
  Use for partnership-tax treatment of multi-member LLCs, including the baseline idea that partnerships pass through income rather than paying entity-level income tax.
- [IRS Publication 542: Corporations](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p542)
  Use for ordinary domestic corporation tax basics, property-for-stock exchanges, corporation basis in contributed property, and high-level corporate reorganization pointers.
- [26 U.S.C. section 351: Transfer to corporation controlled by transferor](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/351)
  Use for the core nonrecognition rule when property is transferred to a corporation solely for stock and the transferors control the corporation immediately afterward.
- [26 U.S.C. section 704: Partner's distributive share](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/704)
  Use for contributed-property allocation issues, especially built-in gain or loss under section 704(c).
- [26 U.S.C. section 707: Transactions between partner and partnership](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/707)
  Use for disguised-sale and partner-capacity questions when a purported contribution is paired with cash or other consideration.
- [26 U.S.C. section 721: Nonrecognition on contribution to partnership](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/721)
  Use for the baseline rule that contribution of property to a partnership for a partnership interest is generally nonrecognition.
- [26 U.S.C. section 731: Recognition on partnership distributions](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/731)
  Use for cash and property distributions that may accompany a rollover or partnership restructuring.
- [26 U.S.C. section 752: Treatment of partnership liabilities](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/752)
  Use for liability shifts that are treated as contributions or distributions of money.
- [26 U.S.C. section 368: Definitions relating to corporate reorganizations](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/368)
  Use for the statutory menu of corporate reorganization forms, including mergers, stock acquisitions, asset acquisitions, divisive reorganizations, recapitalizations, and changes in identity, form, or place.
- [26 C.F.R. section 1.368-2: Definition of reorganization terms](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/26/1.368-2)
  Use for Type F reorganization requirements and examples, including the "mere change" framing.
- [26 C.F.R. section 301.7701-3: Classification of certain business entities](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/26/301.7701-3)
  Use for check-the-box entity classification rules and the deemed steps when an eligible entity changes classification.
- [IRS Practice Unit: Overview of Entity Classification Regulations, aka Check-the-Box](https://www.irs.gov/pub/fatca/int_practice_units/ore_c_19_02_01.pdf)
  Use as an IRS training aid for visualizing the deemed contribution and liquidation steps in classification changes.
- [IRS Topic No. 407: Business Income](https://www.irs.gov/taxtopics/tc407)
  Use for a short current IRS summary of how sole proprietors, partnerships, corporations, and LLCs report business income.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- Tax counsel, CPA, or transaction-tax specialist retained for the specific deal.
  Use for live transaction judgment, because contributions and reorganizations are fact-sensitive and can turn on liabilities, valuation, consideration mix, state law steps, and anti-abuse rules.
