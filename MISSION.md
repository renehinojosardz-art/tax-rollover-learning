# Mission: LLC and C-Corp Contributions and Reorgs

## Why
I want practical federal tax-structure fluency for M&A deals involving tax-free rollovers, equity contributions, LLCs, partnerships, C-corps, fund structures, recapitalizations, and reorganizations. The goal is to understand the mechanics well enough to structure thoughtfully, spot issues early, and draft diligent first-pass memos that frame the right questions for tax counsel and deal teams.

## Success looks like
- Identify whether a deal step is best framed as an LLC or partnership contribution, a section 351 corporate contribution, a check-the-box conversion, a C-corp recapitalization, a Type F reorganization, or another section 368 reorganization.
- Explain the tax mechanics of founder rollovers, equity contributions, partnership-interest contributions, real-estate/property contributions, and fund-structure rollovers in plain English.
- Draft a careful memo section that states the likely tax bucket, relevant deemed steps, principal authorities, assumptions, recognition risks, basis consequences, and diligence questions.
- Build transaction-step checklists for recognition, basis, liabilities, boot, control, continuity, disguised-sale risk, partnership anti-abuse concerns, and reorganization qualification.
- Know when the analysis has crossed from diligent issue spotting into tax-opinion territory that needs specialist review.

## Constraints
- This is a learning workspace for federal tax structure and memo drafting, not final legal or tax advice for any live transaction.
- Use primary and official sources first, then high-quality practitioner materials as supplements.
- Keep lessons short, applied, and deal-pattern driven.
- Favor diagrams, deemed-step walkthroughs, issue checklists, and memo-ready language over abstract tax doctrine.

## Out of scope
- State and local tax, franchise tax, transfer tax, and property-tax reassessment issues unless a specific deal pattern requires a flag.
- International tax, FIRPTA, withholding, tax treaty, and cross-border fund issues unless later added.
- S-corps, tax-exempt investors, ERISA, securities law, accounting, valuation, and financial-modeling issues except as issue-spotting flags.
- Final tax opinions, opinion-level conclusions, or transaction-document drafting without specialist tax counsel review.
