Classify the Transaction First

Lesson 0001 · LLC and C-corp contributions and reorgs

This first lesson teaches one skill: before asking whether gain is recognized, identify the entity's tax classification and the transaction bucket.

The core move: Do not start with "LLC versus corporation" as legal labels. Start with federal tax classification, then ask what moved and what was received back.

The three-step scan

1. Classify the entity Is the LLC disregarded, a partnership, or taxed as a corporation? A domestic LLC's default classification depends on the number of members unless it elects corporation treatment. Source: IRS Pub. 3402.
2. Track what moved Cash, appreciated property, liabilities, services, assets, stock, or mixed consideration can point to different rules and different issues.
3. Pick the bucket Use the closest tax bucket: partnership/LLC contribution, section 351 contribution, check-the-box classification change, or section 368 reorganization.

The four buckets

LLC contribution

An LLC contribution is not a complete tax description until you know how the LLC is classified. A multi-member domestic LLC that does not elect otherwise is generally treated as a partnership for federal income tax purposes. Sources: IRS Pub. 541 and IRS LLC classification guidance.

Section 351 contribution to a C-corp

Section 351 is the starting point when property is transferred to a corporation solely in exchange for stock and the transferor group controls the corporation immediately after the exchange. Source: 26 U.S.C. 351.

Check-the-box conversion

When an eligible entity changes classification, tax law supplies deemed steps. For example, a partnership electing corporation status is deemed to contribute assets and liabilities to a corporation for stock, then distribute that stock in liquidation. Sources: 26 C.F.R. 301.7701-3 and the IRS check-the-box practice unit.

Section 368 reorganization

A reorganization is not a generic restructuring label. Section 368 defines specific reorganization types, including statutory mergers, stock acquisitions, asset acquisitions, recapitalizations, and changes in identity, form, or place. Source: 26 U.S.C. 368.

Practice

Pick the best first bucket. This is about classification, not the final tax answer.

Scenario: Two founders hold membership interests in a domestic LLC taxed as a partnership. They file an entity classification election so the LLC will be treated as a corporation for federal tax purposes. After the election, they own stock in the corporation in the same proportions.

Choose an answer.

What to say out loud

For the practice scenario, a strong first-pass answer is:

The legal entity is an LLC, but the key tax event is a classification change. I would analyze the deemed steps under 26 C.F.R. 301.7701-3(g): the partnership is treated as contributing assets and liabilities to a corporation for stock, then liquidating by distributing the stock to the partners. Then I would ask whether section 351 and liability rules affect recognition and basis.
