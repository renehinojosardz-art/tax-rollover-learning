Rollover Memo Triage

Use this before drafting. The goal is not to reach a final tax conclusion; it is to classify the rollover and identify the memo questions that control the analysis.

Section 721 Property goes to a partnership or LLC taxed as a partnership in exchange for a partnership interest.
Section 351 Property goes to a corporation solely for stock, and the transferor group controls the corporation immediately after.
Section 368(a)(1)(F) A corporation changes identity, form, or place of organization without changing the corporate taxpayer in substance.
Taxable sale plus rollover A seller cashes out part of its position and rolls part into buyer equity or a holding vehicle.

Memo Triage Table

QuestionDrafting purposeAuthorities to pull
What entity receives the rollover property? Separates partnership contribution analysis from corporate contribution or reorganization analysis. section 721; section 351
What is transferred: assets, equity, partnership interests, real estate, or cash? Determines whether the contribution is property, whether look-through concerns exist, and whether built-in gain needs tracking. section 704(c); IRS Pub. 541
What does the rollover party receive back? Stock, partnership interests, cash, notes, or other property can change recognition and memo posture. section 351(b); section 731
Do liabilities shift? Liability assumptions can be treated as money contributions or distributions and may create gain or disguised-sale concerns. section 752; section 707
Is there a pre-closing F reorg or recap? Determines whether the memo must analyze reorganization qualification before the sale or rollover step. section 368(a)(1)(F); Treas. Reg. 1.368-2

Memo Paragraph Skeleton

  1. Classification: "The rollover appears to be best analyzed as..."
  2. Mechanics: "The intended tax mechanics are..."
  3. Authorities: "The principal authorities are..."
  4. Assumptions: "This conclusion assumes..."
  5. Risks: "The main diligence points are..."
Drafting discipline: Do not call a rollover "tax-free" in a memo until you state the bucket, the consideration received, the liability treatment, and the open diligence assumptions.
