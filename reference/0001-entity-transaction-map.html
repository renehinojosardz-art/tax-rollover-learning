Entity Transaction Map

Use this quick map before analyzing tax consequences. The first skill is classification: what transaction bucket are you in?

LLC

LLC contribution

A member contributes cash, property, services, or an obligation to an LLC. For federal tax, first ask how the LLC is classified: disregarded entity, partnership, or corporation.

  • Default domestic single-member LLC: disregarded.
  • Default domestic multi-member LLC: partnership.
  • Election can make an eligible LLC taxable as a corporation.
C-corp

Section 351 contribution

One or more persons transfer property to a corporation solely for stock and control the corporation immediately after the exchange.

  • Core question: property for stock?
  • Control cross-reference: section 368(c).
  • Watch boot, liabilities, services, and disproportionate issuances.
Conversion

Check-the-box change

An eligible entity changes federal tax classification. The regulations often treat the change as a deemed contribution, liquidation, or both.

  • Partnership to corporation: assets and liabilities deemed contributed to a corporation for stock, then partnership liquidates.
  • Disregarded entity to corporation: owner deemed to contribute assets and liabilities for stock.
Reorg

Section 368 reorganization

A corporation-level restructuring that must fit a statutory reorganization type, such as a merger, stock acquisition, asset acquisition, recapitalization, or change in identity, form, or place.

  • Start with the lettered type: A, B, C, D, E, F, or G.
  • Then test consideration, continuity, plan, and distribution mechanics.

First-pass questions

QuestionWhy it mattersPrimary source
What is the entity's federal tax classification before and after? Legal form and tax classification can diverge, especially for LLCs. IRS Pub. 3402; 26 C.F.R. 301.7701-3
Is property moving to a corporation in exchange for stock? This is the entry point for section 351 nonrecognition analysis. 26 U.S.C. 351
Are assets, stock, liabilities, or cash also moving? Boot, liabilities, and mixed consideration can change recognition and basis. IRS Pub. 542
Does the transaction claim to be a reorganization? A reorg is not just any restructuring; it must fit a statutory type and related requirements. 26 U.S.C. 368
Rule of thumb: classify the entity first, then classify the transaction. Many mistakes come from treating an LLC as if its legal form alone tells you the federal tax result.
